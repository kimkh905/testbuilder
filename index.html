Study Path

A beginner roadmap that feels manageable.

Week 1

Learn Hangul structure

Focus on how Korean letters combine into blocks. Recognize consonants, vowels, and final sounds before chasing difficult grammar.

Week 2

Use survival phrases

Practice greetings, thanks, apologies, and polite requests that you can immediately use in Korea or with Korean friends.

Week 3

Talk about yourself

Learn name, country, job, and daily routine phrases so you can build real introductions instead of isolated vocabulary.

Week 4

Build listening stamina

Repeat short dialogues, shadow natural audio, and review the same phrases until your ears stop treating Korean like noise.

Culture Notes

Language lands better when you understand the tone behind it.

Polite speech matters

Beginners should usually learn the polite style first. It sounds safer, more respectful, and works in most daily situations.

Name + 이에요/예요

Self-introduction in Korean is often softer and more formulaic than direct English introductions. Repetition here is useful, not boring.

Listening before speed

Fast speaking is not the first goal. Clear pronunciation and rhythm will help you more than memorizing 500 words badly.
